Thank you for your interest in TeCanal.

Three years ago, TeCanal was founded with the mission of using technology and STEM education to empower youth in low-income communities. Today, TeCanal serves over seventy children in some of the most impoverished communities in Maryland each year.

TeCanal is looking for new executive officers to lead TeCanal during the 2018-2019 academic year. You must be a rising sophomore, junior, or senior in high school to apply. All executive officers will be expected to volunteer at outreach sessions in Baltimore, MD regularly. Additionally, executive officers will have meetings at least twice a month. Some of these will be online, and some of these will be in-person in Howard County, MD.

We sincerely hope to appoint a new suite of executive officers who believe in the power of technology and STEM education in making our world a better place. Our vision is a world where everyone has the technology resources and STEM education they need to achieve their potential. We encourage you to be part of our next generation of leaders and help make TeCanal's vision come true!

Applications will close on Friday, May 25 at 11:59 PM. Late applications will not be accepted.

If you have any questions about the application or TeCanal in general, please email application@tecanal.org. A member of the board of directors will reply as soon as possible.

Positions Available for 2018 - 2019:

  • CEO
  • COO
  • CFO
  • CTO
  • CHRO
  • CMO

CEO (Chief Executive Officer)

Roles:
  • Lead and manage all of the executive officers
  • Direct major operations
  • Communicate with the Board of Directors on all of the company's actions and strategies
  • Act as the figurehead and leader of the organization
  • Represent the organization in all formal and public relations when necessary

COO: Chief Operating Officer

Roles:
  • Create a suitable outreach expansion and development plan at the beginning of the Fiscal Year (January)
  • Plan outreach sessions
  • File a report on each of the outreach sessions
  • Coordinate with outreach directors, volunteers, students, and other executives
  • Lead development of lesson plans and teaching strategies
  • Find other ways to provide technology and/or STEM education to low-income neighborhoods

CFO: Chief Financial Officer

Roles:
  • Create budget at beginning of fiscal year (January)
  • Calculate total income received and money spent at end of fiscal year
  • Make decisions on how to spend money
  • Lead fundraising programs
  • Help apply for grants
  • Contact business and government leaders to request funding

CTO: Chief Technology Officer

Roles:
  • Discuss operations and curriculum with the COO
  • Manage and direct the maintenance, purchase and usage of all technical equipment in the organization
  • Develop new technical resources for the organization
  • Employ talent to help with the development, management and organization of technical resources-the CTO will work in conjunction with the CHRO for this role
  • Manage the technical aspects not directly related to the outreach operations such as websites, applications, and/or administrative software

CHRO: Chief Human Resources Officer

Roles:
  • Keep meticulous records of who is volunteering on what specific dates
  • Recruit new volunteers from other schools
  • Keep track of and communicating with both the Board of Director and the CEO any disciplinary problems reported by outreach students, fellow volunteers, or any other individuals who interact with TeCanal in other capacities
  • Act as a formal liaison between volunteers and the organization
  • Track hours of volunteers for Presidential Volunteer Service Award

CMO: Chief Marketing Officer

Roles:
  • Oversee and maintain TeCanal's presence on a variety of social media platforms
  • Maintain a monthly newsletter
  • Facilite a positive relationship with local news media, including the publicization of our events and the dispersion of our message.
  • Work in committee to develop a clear and marketable presence for our organization
  • Act as a liaison between the public and the organization
